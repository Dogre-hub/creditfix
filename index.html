FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to the most common questions about credit repair

How much can you improve my credit score?

Our clients typically see improvements of 100-200+ points within 60-90 days. The exact improvement depends on your current credit profile, the number of negative items, and how quickly creditors respond to disputes. We've helped clients increase their scores by as much as 250 points.

How long does the credit repair process take?

Most clients see initial results within 30-45 days, with significant improvements by 60-90 days. Complex cases may take 4-6 months for maximum results. We provide monthly updates so you can track your progress throughout the process.

Is credit repair legal?

Yes, credit repair is completely legal. The Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) gives you the right to dispute inaccurate, outdated, or unverifiable information on your credit report. We use only legal methods and comply with all federal regulations.

What's included in your money-back guarantee?

If we don't remove any negative items or improve your credit score within 120 days, we'll refund 100% of your money. No questions asked. We're confident in our process and stand behind our results.

Can you remove bankruptcies and foreclosures?

While bankruptcies and foreclosures are serious negative marks, we can often remove them if there are inaccuracies in how they're reported, procedural errors, or if they're past the legal reporting period. Each case is unique, and we'll analyze your specific situation.

Do you work with all three credit bureaus?

Yes, we work with Experian, Equifax, and TransUnion. We also communicate directly with creditors and collection agencies when necessary. Our comprehensive approach ensures improvements across all your credit reports.
